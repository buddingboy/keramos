Keramos

A joint venture of Indian Ceramic Society, Student chapter and Department of Ceramic Engineering IIT BHU, Keramos promises a feat beyond comparisons. The student chapter, Indian Ceramic Society (IIT BHU) is all set to bring you a plethora of events, and a spectacle worth waiting for. The first edition saw a vast external participation competing with home teams in a wide range of technical events. After an exemplary success last year, Keramos returns to set the bar even higher, upholding the 91 year old legacy of the Ceramic Department, in a bid to provide the perfect stage for participants to showcase their technical skills and win loads of cash prizes.

Dept. of Ceramic Engineering

The founder of Banaras Hindu University, Pandit Madan Mohan Malviya instituted a course in Ceramic Technology as early as 1924 with the gallant objective for the advancement of glass and ceramic technology in India. In the year 1956, Department of Glass Technology was merged to form Department of Silicate Technology which further was renamed as Department of Ceramic Engineering making it one of the oldest departments in the then Institute of Technology. In 2012 Institute of Technology joined the elite group of IITs and thus IIT (BHU) Varanasi became the only IIT to offer B.Tech, M.Tech and PhD programmes in Ceramic Engineering and Technology. The department has since been producing pioneers in the field of ceramics with comprehensive scientific and technological background. Ceramic Engineering department at IIT BHU spearheads scientific research in the field of nanotechnology, electro-ceramics, bio-ceramics and other contemporary and traditional facets of ceramics.
